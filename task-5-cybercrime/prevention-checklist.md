# Online Safety Checklist for Indian College Students
- Use strong, unique passwords for each account (mix letters,numbers, symbols) and enable two-factor authentication (2FA).
- Keep your apps and devices updated to protect against security vulnerabilities, use Anti-Virus and malware detecting software.
- Verify links before clicking – especially in emails, WhatsApp, or SMS messages claiming offers, jobs, or urgent alerts.
- Limit personal information shared online.
- Download apps only from trusted sources like official app stores and check reviews/permissions.
- Log out from shared/public devices.
- Never approve unknown “collect requests” on apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm— you send money when you approve, double-check receiver details before sending money, especially during online shopping or renting/booking deals.
- Avoid using public Wi-Fi for banking or payments without a secure connection (VPN).
## If You Face Cyber Fraud:

- Report immediately on the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in)
- Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (available across India for quick action)
